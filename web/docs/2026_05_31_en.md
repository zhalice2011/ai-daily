[Build agents, not pipelines](https://seangoedecke.com/build-agents-not-pipelines/) — seangoedecke.com · 05-31 00:00 · 🤖 AI / ML

> Testing whether two polynomials are identical can be done efficiently by evaluating them at random points, leveraging the Schwartz-Zippel lemma. The lemma states that if two distinct polynomials of degree d over a finite field are evaluated at a random point, the probability of equality is at most d/|F|. This approach is useful for verifying identities like binomial coefficient formulas or polynomial equalities in computer algebra systems. The method is probabilistic but can achieve high confidence with few random tests. The author concludes that random spot-checking is a practical and reliable technique for polynomial identity testing.

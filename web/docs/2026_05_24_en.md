### 7. Hilbert transform as an infinite matrix

[Hilbert transform as an infinite matrix](https://www.johndcook.com/blog/2026/05/23/hilbert-transform-as-an-infinite-matrix/) — **johndcook.com** · 05-23 15:09 · ⭐ 16/30

> The Hilbert transform maps a function's Fourier series of sine and cosine terms to a new series where the coefficients are swapped and sign-changed. This operation can be represented as an infinite matrix acting on the Fourier coefficient vector. The matrix has a block structure, with each 2x2 block rotating the coefficients of each frequency. This perspective linearizes the Hilbert transform, making it easier to analyze in signal processing contexts.

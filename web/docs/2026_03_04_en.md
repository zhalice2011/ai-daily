> The Intel 486DX2, introduced on March 3, 1992, was the first clock-multiplied x86 CPU, marking a key advancement in microprocessor technology. It was a clock-doubled version of the earlier 486, running at speeds of 50 or 66 MHz with a front-side bus of 25 or 33 MHz, respectively. This innovation allowed for higher internal clock speeds while maintaining compatibility with existing motherboard designs, improving performance without full system upgrades. The 486DX2's architecture enabled more efficient processing by doubling the internal clock relative to the external bus. Its introduction paved the way for future CPU developments using clock multiplication techniques.

### 7. The Laplace limit

[The Laplace limit](https://www.johndcook.com/blog/2026/06/07/the-laplace-limit/) — **johndcook.com** · 06-07 19:06 · ⭐ 9/30

> Kepler's equation M = E - e sin E can be solved using a power series in eccentricity e, as Lagrange did in 1771, or a sine series as Bessel did. The power series solution converges only for e less than the Laplace limit, approximately 0.6627. Beyond this limit, the series diverges, marking a fundamental boundary in celestial mechanics. The Laplace limit arises from the radius of convergence of the series expansion, determined by the complex singularities of the inverse Kepler equation.
